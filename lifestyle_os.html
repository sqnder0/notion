Module 03
DAILY RULES
The protocols that run every day. Consistency at this level compounds faster than any single hack or optimization.
Wake & Sleep Protocol
06:45
Alarm — No Snooze, Ever
One alarm. Snoozing fragments sleep cycles and spikes cortisol chaotically. Set it. Get up. The discipline is the point.
06:45
Hydration First
400–500ml water immediately. You lose ~1L overnight. Cognitive performance degrades with even mild dehydration.
06:50
Light Exposure
Open curtains or go outside immediately. Morning sunlight into your eyes (no sunglasses) anchors your circadian rhythm and peaks morning cortisol — which is how you feel alert and focused. This is the highest ROI habit in this document.
07:00
Breakfast — Protein First
High protein, moderate carbs. See Nutrition module. No phone during breakfast — eat, think, plan your day mentally for 10 minutes.
07:20
Commute / Brief Review
During commute: either mental review of what you need to accomplish today, OR passive audio (podcast, audio lecture). Not social media. Not passive scrolling. Your commute is 30 min of daily prime time — use it.
21:30
Evening Wind-Down Starts
Screen brightness drops. Blue light filter on. No new stimulating content (arguments, intense videos, stressful planning). This is your transition window.
22:00
Phone Out of Bedroom
Non-negotiable. Buy a cheap alarm clock if needed. The phone being present in the bedroom is a proven sleep disruptor — even if you don't use it, knowing it's there increases arousal.
22:30
Lights Out
Target. Tuesday is an exception (volleyball ends at 22:00 + 30min transit = 22:30 home → sleep by 23:15). On Tuesday nights, sleep takes priority over any "review" or business impulse.
Study Method — How, Not Just When

The fundamental error: Most students study by re-reading notes. This is the lowest-ROI study method that exists. It produces familiarity, not memory. You need to study in a way that creates retrieval practice — which means testing yourself, not reviewing material.

01
Active Recall over Passive Review. Close your notes. Write out everything you know about a topic from memory. Then check. The gap between what you wrote and the source is where learning happens.
02
Pomodoro 50/10. 50 minutes of single-subject focus, 10-minute break. After 3 cycles, take a 30-minute break. Study one thing per cycle — no switching subjects mid-session.
03
Phone in another room during study. Not on silent. Not face-down. In another room. Presence of a phone on your desk reduces cognitive capacity by ~20% even when not used (research-proven).
04
Spaced repetition for memorization. For content that must be memorized (definitions, formulas, vocabulary), use Anki or a similar app. 15 minutes daily beats 2 hours of cramming before an exam.
05
End each session with a 3-minute brain dump. Write out the 3 most important concepts you just studied without looking. This cements long-term memory more effectively than any review.

TRAINING SYSTEM
Your current split is broken. Here's a direct assessment and a better system built around hormonal output, athletic performance, and your actual schedule.

PROBLEM WITH YOUR CURRENT SPLIT (Core / Pull / Core / Push):

1. No leg work. Squats and deadlifts are the highest testosterone-stimulating exercises in existence. Skipping legs for "core" days is the single biggest training mistake you can make if testosterone is a priority.

2. Two core days is overkill. The core is trained as a stabilizer in every compound movement. Dedicated core sessions are largely redundant and waste recovery capacity.

3. One push day is insufficient for a volleyball athlete who needs explosive shoulder and chest strength. You need more pressing volume.

4. The split doesn't account for volleyball load. Wednesday (5h coaching+training) and Tuesday (2h training) are high-fatigue days. The sessions after these need to be structured accordingly.

Testosterone Optimization Rules for Training
01
Compound lifts first, always. Squat, Deadlift, Bench, Row, Overhead Press. These are the movements that trigger the largest hormonal response. Do them at the start of every session, never as accessories.
02
Rep ranges 3–6 for heavy compounds, 8–12 for accessories. Heavy compound loading (85%+ 1RM) produces the highest acute testosterone spike. Don't do 15-rep sets on squats and expect a hormonal response.
03
60–90 second rest on accessories, 2–3 min on compounds. Insufficient rest → cortisol spike → testosterone suppression. Rest properly on heavy sets.
04
Sessions cap at 75–80 minutes maximum. Beyond 75–90 min, cortisol rises sharply and testosterone falls. Longer gym sessions are not better — they're often worse. Get in, execute, get out.
05
Monthly deload: reduce volume 40% for one week. Every 4–6 weeks. Do not skip this. Chronic training without recovery accumulates hidden fatigue that tanks performance and hormones simultaneously.
06
Face pulls every pull session — no exceptions. Volleyball is an overhead sport. Heavy pressing without posterior shoulder work = impingement within 18 months. 3×15 Face Pulls. Always.
Volleyball Load Integration — Overtraining Prevention

Wednesday is a 5-hour volleyball block. This must be treated as a full training session for recovery purposes. The mistake most student-athletes make is thinking "I only did volleyball, not the gym" — your body does not distinguish. 5 hours of court sport is demanding. Thursday gym should never be maximal effort — it should be a controlled push session with abbreviated rest times and no intensity PR attempts.

NUTRITION SYSTEM
Not a diet plan. A set of rules for meals you control, optimized for testosterone, performance, and simplicity. No calorie counting. No meal prep obsession.

THE BIGGEST NUTRITION MISTAKE FOR TESTOSTERONE: Eating too little fat. The most pervasive "healthy eating" myth is that fat is bad. Testosterone is synthesized from cholesterol. A low-fat diet directly suppresses testosterone production. Your diet must include adequate dietary fat daily — from eggs, meat, nuts, olive oil, and dairy. If you've been eating low-fat, this one change will have a measurable impact.

Breakfast Upgrade — Controlled Meal #1

Your current breakfast: oatmeal + milk. Assessment: good carb base, but critically low in protein and fat. Testosterone production requires both. This meal is incomplete.

UPGRADED BREAKFAST TEMPLATE
①
Keep the oats. 60–80g oats + milk or water. Slow carbs for sustained energy. No change needed here.
②
Add 3 whole eggs. Scrambled, poached, or fried. Eggs are the most complete testosterone food in existence — cholesterol, zinc, vitamin D, complete protein. Non-negotiable upgrade.
③
Add a handful of nuts. Almonds, walnuts, or Brazil nuts (Brazil nuts contain selenium — a known testosterone cofactor). 30g. Takes 10 seconds to add.
④
Optional: 1 banana. Potassium + B6, which supports testosterone synthesis. Easy, cheap, portable.
RESULT: ~40–45g protein, ~60g carbs, ~20g fat. Hormonal, satiating, no crash.
Lunch Structure — Controlled Meal #2

1/3

Dinner — Uncontrolled Meal Strategy

02
One rule: always add protein if possible. If dinner is carb-heavy, add an egg or some meat. If it's genuinely lacking protein, have a protein shake after.
03
Eat dinner ≥ 2 hours before bed when possible. Heavy eating close to sleep time disrupts sleep quality and growth hormone release. This is worth navigating within your household constraints.

Module 06
ENERGY MANAGEMENT
Burnout is not caused by doing too much. It's caused by doing too much without recovery. Here's how to sustain an elite schedule without breaking.
Burnout Prevention — Early Warning System

You will not feel burnout coming until it's already happening. By that point, recovery takes weeks, not days. The system needs proactive recovery built in, not reactive rest after collapse. These signals tell you to pull back before the collapse:

⚠
Persistent morning grogginess despite 7.5h sleep — your body is under-recovered, not just tired from one bad night.
⚠
Training sessions that feel inexplicably hard — when weights that were easy last week feel crushing, it's not motivation. It's systemic fatigue.
⚠
Loss of interest in business or study — apathy toward goals you normally care about is a cortisol-dominance symptom, not laziness.
⚠
Irritability in relationship or with teammates — social friction increases when your nervous system is overloaded. This is a body signal, not a personality problem.
⚠
Getting sick frequently — chronic stress suppresses immune function. More than 2 colds in a season = your recovery is broken.
Recovery Protocols
01
Strategic napping: 15–20 min, not more. On days with afternoon windows (post-school, pre-volleyball), a 20-minute nap restores alertness equivalent to ~1–1.5h extra sleep. Set an alarm. 20 min max — longer creates sleep inertia and tanks evening performance.
02
Saturday is your recovery anchor. Drawing academy is mentally creative, not draining. After 12:30, no performance obligations. Protect this space. If you consistently fill Saturdays with "productivity," you'll be coasting on fumes by Thursday every week.
03
Monthly training deload. Every 4–6 weeks, reduce gym volume by 40%. Keep intensity but drop sets. This is not laziness — it's when your nervous system and hormonal system actually supercompensate. Most gains happen during deloads, not during peak training weeks.
04
Cold-warm shower contrast. After intense training sessions, 3 min warm → 1 min cold → 1 min warm → 30s cold. Reduces systemic inflammation, accelerates muscle recovery, and raises alertness for the rest of the day without caffeine.
05
No caffeine after 13:00. Caffeine's half-life is ~5–7 hours. Coffee at 15:00 = half a cup at 21:00. Even if you fall asleep fine, caffeine degrades sleep quality and reduces deep sleep cycles — which is where testosterone is primarily produced.
Stress Management — Cortisol Control

Testosterone and cortisol are inversely related. When cortisol is chronically elevated — from poor sleep, overtraining, academic stress, relationship friction, or constant stimulation — testosterone drops. This is not a motivational issue. It is biochemistry. Your OS must manage cortisol as actively as it manages training load.

01
10-min evening walk. Post-dinner, no phone. Walking after eating improves insulin sensitivity and significantly reduces cortisol. Sounds minor — compounds over months.
02
No decision-making after 21:00. Willpower and decision quality decline sharply in the evening. Decisions made when fatigued generate cortisol and regret in equal measure. Schedule tomorrow before you wind down, then stop making decisions.
03
Drawing academy is your cortisol relief valve. Creative work that isn't performance-measured is deeply restorative. Don't treat it as something to optimize or rush through — it's actively recovering your nervous system.
Module 07
BUSINESS SYSTEM
Building and selling websites is a real business. Treat it as one. The number one reason side businesses fail isn't lack of time — it's treating the time like it doesn't count.

HARD TRUTH: If you work on your business "whenever I have time," you will never build anything real. Every serious person who built something while studying or working did it through protected, scheduled, mandatory blocks — not through motivation and spare hours. Motivation is not a scheduling strategy. Time-blocking is.

Business Block Architecture
WED
Business Block #1 — 12:00 to 14:30
Your best weekly window. School ends 11:50. Volleyball prep starts 14:30. You have 2.5 focused hours. This is deep work time — no admin, no social media "research," no casual browsing. Build something, contact someone, close something.
SUN
Business Block #2 — 17:00 to 19:00
Post-gym recovery, then 2h of focused business work. Earlier in the day works if gym is done by 11:00. This block is protected — if matches are on Sunday, shift to earlier morning (08:00–10:00) before the match.
SAT
Optional: Administrative block — 13:00 to 14:00
After drawing academy. Use for low-cognitive business tasks only: emails, invoices, portfolio updates, client communication follow-ups. Not for deep building or coding. Protect deeper Saturday time for girlfriend or genuine rest.
Website Business — Operating Model
01
Productize your service immediately. "I build websites" is not a business. "I build 5-page professional websites for local tradespeople in 7 days, starting at €X" is a business. Narrow scope. Fixed deliverable. Clear price. You can sell the first; you can scale the second.
02
Your first 3 clients are the entire game right now. Don't build a perfect portfolio site. Don't brand yourself. Don't set up complex tooling. Get 3 paying clients. Show their results. That is your portfolio. Everything else is procrastination with extra steps.
03
Outreach is a non-negotiable in every business block. If you only build sites and never reach out, you're building a hobby. Every block should include at least 10 minutes of outreach — cold email, Instagram DM, LinkedIn, local business visits. The pipeline must always be moving.
04
Build with leverage — templates, components, page builders. You are time-constrained. Custom code from scratch for €800 is not viable. Use Webflow, Framer, or WordPress with a premium theme. Build a reusable template system. Deliver in 5–7 days. This is where the business becomes scalable.
05
Weekly business goal: one specific output. Every Monday morning, write one concrete deliverable for the week: "Contact 20 local restaurants" or "Finish homepage for client X" or "Send 3 proposals." Not a mood, not a direction. A deliverable.
Focus Rules for Business Sessions
—
Write your single task for the session before it starts. Not "work on business." Something like: "Write 3 outreach emails to local restaurants."
—
Phone away. Email closed. Only the tool you need for the task is open.
—
Timer on for the full block. When the timer ends, stop — even mid-sentence. This trains your brain to work with urgency within the window.
—
At session end, write tomorrow's first action. Never leave a business session without knowing exactly what comes next. The biggest momentum killer is starting the next session by trying to remember where you were.
Module 08
RELATIONSHIP INTEGRATION
A relationship that supports your goals is an asset. One that drains without boundaries is a liability. The difference is almost never about the person — it's about the structure.
Scheduled Time Architecture

The counterintuitive truth about relationships in a high-performance schedule: scheduled time beats spontaneous time. Spontaneous time often means anxious time — you're half-present because you're aware of what you "should" be doing. Scheduled, protected time means you show up fully because you've already decided this is where your attention belongs right now.

01
Saturday is the primary relationship day. Drawing academy ends at 12:30. The afternoon and evening are for your girlfriend. Suggest this structure to her explicitly — not as a constraint, but as a commitment: "Saturday afternoon is always yours."
02
Define Sunday's role in advance. Sunday has gym, study, and business. It should not be a default relationship day unless you've planned for it. Transparency here prevents friction — if she expects Sunday and you're deep in a study block, that's a conflict that could have been avoided.
03
Short, high-quality contact during the week beats nothing. You don't need to see her every evening. A 20-minute genuine conversation during your free window beats a distracted 2-hour visit where you're mentally elsewhere. Quality of attention outweighs quantity of time.
The Presence Rule

When it's her time, your phone is away and your ambitions are parked. Not because the business doesn't matter — but because the ability to be present is itself a high-performance skill. People who cannot disconnect and be present are not high performers. They're anxious. The goal is to be able to fully switch modes. Work → Present. Gym → Present. Relationship → Present.

Long-Term Sustainability Rules
01
Communicate the OS, not the results. Tell her what you're building and why the schedule matters. A partner who understands the "why" becomes an ally. A partner who only sees the "you're always busy" becomes friction.
02
Protect her time when you said you would. Reliability is the core competency here. Don't cancel Saturday plans for "an important business thing" unless it's genuinely exceptional. Patterns of cancellation destroy trust faster than any single incident.
03
Review the OS together quarterly. As your schedule shifts (exams, tournaments, client deadlines), recalibrate what the relationship time looks like. Relationships that don't adapt to changing life phases generate silent resentment.
04
A healthy relationship reduces stress — it doesn't add it. If your relationship is consistently a source of anxiety, distraction, or guilt, that is a cortisol problem as much as it is an emotional one. The solution is not more time sacrificed — it's better structure, clearer communication, and more reliable presence when you are there.
