Priority Hierarchy — Conflict Resolution Law
1
Sleep — 8h minimum, non-negotiable
Every other goal degrades without sleep. Testosterone synthesis, study retention, athletic recovery — all depend on this. If you must trade sleep for anything, you lose more than you gain. The only exception is Tuesday volleyball which forces a 23:00 sleep — acceptable once per week.
2
Fixed commitments — school + volleyball coaching
You coach people on Wednesday. They depend on you. You cannot skip. School is your primary institutional obligation. These are locked before anything else is scheduled.
3
Gym sessions — treat as unbreakable appointments
The testosterone and athletic performance goals only hold if training is consistent. A skipped gym session is not recovered later — it simply doesn't happen. However: gym can be shortened on low-energy days. It cannot be skipped.
4
Deep study — scheduled, protected blocks only
Study happens in designated blocks, never "when I have time." The moment study becomes optional, it gets displaced. Each block has a pre-assigned subject. No block = no cognitive work that day.
5
Reading + business + relationship
These are real priorities, but they fit into the remaining space after 1–4 are honored. Reading fills evenings and Saturday. Business gets Wednesday midday and Sunday. Relationship gets Saturday afternoon/evening.
Energy Law — The Most Important Scheduling Rule
E1
Cognitive peak = 07:00–12:00. Morning cortisol is highest in the first 2–4 hours after waking. This is when your brain is fastest and retention is highest. Deep study always goes here when possible. Thursday morning (07:45–09:15) is your single best cognitive window all week — it has no competition from school.
E2
Post-school window (15:30–17:00) = moderate. Energy is down after 6–8h of school. One focused study block here is realistic. Two back-to-back blocks are not. Use this for active recall (lower cognitive overhead) rather than new material learning.
E3
Post-gym/sport = no deep work. After gym, your CNS is taxed. After volleyball, your body is exhausted and cortisol is suppressed. Light review (Anki, re-reading notes) is acceptable. New material acquisition is a waste of time after intense physical output.
E4
Post-Wednesday coaching = zero cognitive load. 5 hours of coaching + commuting = system depleted. Do not attempt any study or business on Wednesday evening. This is a recovery block. Force this rule even when you feel "fine."
E5
Sunday morning = second best cognitive window. No school pressure, fresh from Saturday rest, gym not yet done. Sunday 07:45–09:15 is your second premium study block. Use it for the hardest subject of the week.

This is where business gets 90 minutes of uncompromised focus, not squeezed between fatigue and sleep.

Module 04 / Performance
TRAINING SYSTEM
Your current Core/Pull/Core/Push split is broken. Here is a direct diagnosis and a replacement that accounts for volleyball load.

WHAT'S WRONG WITH YOUR CURRENT SPLIT:

1. No legs. Squats and deadlifts are the highest testosterone-stimulating movements that exist. Skipping them entirely while prioritizing "core days" is the most counterproductive choice you can make if hormonal optimization is a goal.

2. Two core days. The core is trained as a stabilizer in every compound movement. Isolated core sessions have near-zero testosterone signal and waste recovery capacity that could be used for legs or pressing volume.

3. It doesn't account for volleyball. Wednesday is 5h of coaching — explosive jumping, lateral movement, repeated acceleration. That is a de facto lower-body training day. Thursday gym (the day after) must be upper body only, not legs.

Optimized 4-Day Split
DaySessionPrimary MovementsVolleyball ConsiderationDuration
MONDAY Upper Pull Weighted Pull-Ups (primary, 5×4–6), Barbell Row, Cable Row, Face Pulls 3×15, Bicep Curl. Core: Dead Bug 3×10. Sunday was Lower Heavy → legs need rest. Upper Pull is ideal recovery management. 70 min
THURSDAY Upper Push Bench Press OR OHP (alternate weeks), Incline DB Press, Lateral Raises 4×15, Tricep Dips/Pushdowns, Face Pulls 2×15. Day after 5h coaching. NO LEG WORK. Upper push only. Keep intensity controlled — no PR attempts on Thursdays. 70 min
FRIDAY Lower Athletic Deadlift 4×4 (or Trap Bar), Goblet Squat 3×10, Hip Thrust 3×12, Jump Squat 3×8, Single-Leg Work. Bridges strength + volleyball explosiveness. 2 days post-coaching. Legs partially recovered. This session emphasizes explosiveness, not maximal loading. Moderate intensity. 70 min
SUNDAY Lower Heavy ★ Back Squat 5×3–5 (heaviest session), Romanian Deadlift 3×8, Bulgarian Split Squat 3×8, Leg Curl, Calf Raises. Core finisher: Hanging Leg Raise 3×12. Best recovery state of the week. Saturday = full rest (no gym, no coaching). Sunday = freshest for maximum loading. This is your testosterone-primary session. 85 min
Training Rules
01
Compounds first, always. Squat, deadlift, bench, row, OHP. These are done at the start of every session before any accessory work.
02
Rep ranges: 3–5 reps for heavy compounds, 8–12 for accessories. Heavy loading drives the hormonal response.
03
Rest periods: 2–3 min on compounds, 60–90s on accessories. Rushing rest on compound lifts tanks the quality of subsequent sets.
04
Sessions cap at 80 min. Beyond this, cortisol rises and testosterone drops. Efficiency beats duration.
05
Face Pulls every session. Volleyball is an overhead sport. Posterior shoulder health requires 3×15 face pulls every single training day. Non-negotiable injury prevention.
Deload Protocol
—
Every 4–6 weeks: Reduce volume by 40%. Keep the same weights, cut sets by 40%. Do not skip deloads.
—
Volleyball weeks with matches: Treat heavy match weekends as a soft deload for gym. Drop Sunday's Lower Heavy to Lower Moderate.
—
Sick or genuinely exhausted: Skip gym, never skip sleep. One missed gym session costs nothing. Pushing through illness costs a week of training.
—
Progress tracking: Log main lifts every session. You cannot optimize what you don't measure.
Module 05 / Fuel
NUTRITION SYSTEM
No supplements. No complicated tracking. Simple, repeatable rules that work in a student's real life and directly support testosterone and performance.

THE SINGLE BIGGEST TESTOSTERONE NUTRITION ERROR: Eating low fat. Testosterone is synthesized from cholesterol. A diet that avoids dietary fat directly suppresses testosterone production. If you've been eating "healthy" by avoiding eggs, meat fat, nuts, or whole dairy — this single correction will have a measurable hormonal effect within 4–6 weeks.

Breakfast — Controlled, Optimized

Current: oatmeal + milk. Assessment: good carb base, critically inadequate in protein and fat. Incomplete for testosterone support.

①
Keep the oats. 60–80g oats + milk or water. Slow-release carbs, no change needed.
②
Add 3 whole eggs. Non-negotiable upgrade. Eggs contain cholesterol (testosterone precursor), zinc, vitamin D, and complete protein. Scramble them while oats cook — 5 minutes total prep.
③
Add 30g mixed nuts. Almonds, walnuts, Brazil nuts (selenium — testosterone cofactor). Grab a handful. 10 seconds of effort.
④
Optional: 1 banana. B6 supports testosterone synthesis. Easy to eat on commute.
⑤
Overnight prep on school days. Prep oats the night before (overnight oats). Hard-boil eggs in batches Sunday evening. Breakfast becomes 5-minute assembly, not cooking.
⑥
Result: ~40g protein, ~65g carbs, ~22g fat. Satiating until lunch, no energy crash, hormonal profile aligned.

Module 06 / Risk Analysis
FAILURE POINTS
Where this plan breaks. Predicted in advance. With fixes pre-built in.
Failure Point 01 — Tuesday Sleep Deficit

THE RISK: Volleyball ends at 22:00. Commute home = 22:30. Wind-down is minimal. Sleep at 23:00–23:15. Wake at 07:00 = 7h45 of sleep. Do this repeatedly and testosterone drops, cognitive performance falls, athletic recovery degrades over the week.

FIX
Wednesday sleep extends to 9h to compensate. Sleep at 22:00 on Wednesday. This is already built into the schedule. Tuesday's deficit must be paid back Wednesday — not accumulated. Do not let Wednesday slip past 22:15 bedtime under any circumstances.
Failure Point 02 — Wednesday Evening Study Creep

THE RISK: You come home after 5h of coaching, feeling like you "wasted" the evening. You open your notes. You read for 90 minutes. You retain nothing. You sleep at midnight. You wake up already behind.

FIX
Hard rule: zero cognitive work Wednesday after 20:30. 30 min of light reading (fiction or non-study material) maximum. The business block (12:20–14:00) is the only productive output on Wednesdays. Accept this. The system is designed around it.
Failure Point 03 — Thursday Morning Alarm Resistance

THE RISK: Thursday has no school until 09:55. The alarm sounds at 07:00. Your brain says "I can sleep until 09:00." You do. You lose your best study block of the week. Repeatedly. The entire Thursday architecture collapses.

FIX
Same alarm, every day, including Thursday. Consistent circadian rhythm is itself a testosterone optimizer. The variable of "what time do I wake up today" is a small friction that erodes discipline across all 7 days. One alarm time: 07:00. The 90 minutes before school on Thursday is the payoff for not sleeping in.
Failure Point 04 — Study Without Method

THE RISK: You sit at your desk for 60 minutes. You re-read your notes. You highlight. You feel productive. You retain ~20% of what you reviewed. This is the most common form of academic self-deception. You are experiencing familiarity, not learning.

FIX
Every deep study block begins with a closed-book brain dump. Write everything you know about today's topic before opening any material. The gap between what you wrote and what's in the notes = the actual learning to do. Active recall produces 3–4× the retention of passive review in the same time. The 7h of this schedule executed with active recall beats 12h of re-reading.
Failure Point 05 — Business Block Fragmentation

THE RISK: Wednesday 13:00–14:30 becomes "catch up on emails, scroll for inspiration, tweak the portfolio, do a bit of everything." Nothing ships. No client contacted. No page built. 90 minutes evaporates with the feeling of being busy.

FIX
Define the single output before the block begins. Written on paper before you open the computer: "Build the hero section for [client] site" or "Send outreach emails to 10 local restaurants." One output. If it's done early, start the next one on a pre-defined list. Never begin a business block without knowing exactly what it produces.
Failure Point 06 — Overtraining Accumulation

THE RISK: By week 4–6 without a deload, performance plateaus, mood drops, morning motivation disappears, sleep quality degrades despite adequate duration, and you start blaming "motivation" when the real issue is accumulated systemic fatigue.

FIX
Schedule the deload before you need it. Put it in the calendar: every 5th week, gym volume drops 40%. Same sessions, same days, fewer sets. Volleyball continues normally. This is the most counterintuitive high-performance principle: the scheduled rest week is where the adaptation from the previous 4 weeks actually consolidates. Your next training block will start stronger because of it.
Failure Point 07 — Weekend Match Schedule Collision

THE RISK: Match timing is unpredictable. A Saturday 10:00 match wipes out the reading block and compresses everything. A Sunday 10:00 match eliminates the morning study block — your second best cognitive window of the week.

FIX
Sunday match = Sunday study moves to pre-gym (06:45–08:00 compressed). Saturday match = reading moves to Sunday. The blocks are not deleted — they are shuffled. The non-negotiable is that the blocks happen somewhere in the weekend, not that they happen at the exact time shown.
The One Metric That Tells You If The System Is Working

Ignore weekly output totals. Ignore how many Pomodoros you did. Ignore whether you hit the exact schedule. Ask one question every Sunday evening: "Did I protect my sleep every night this week?"

If yes: the rest of the system is functioning. Training quality holds. Study retention holds. Hormonal state holds. Business output holds.

If no: nothing else in the schedule matters until sleep is fixed first. This is not a motivational frame. It is the central feedback loop of the entire OS.
